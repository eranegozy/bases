Devotion Math League Bases 3: Converting to Base 10

Steps for Converting a Number from a Different Base to Base 10

Example: Convert \( 213_{(4)} \) to Base 10

Step1: Draw a "Base 4 Conversion Table" with exponents on top.
In this case, we make 3 columns because \( 213_{(4)} \) is a 3-digit number.

Step2: Calculate the exponents. These are the place values .

Step3: Put in the digits of the number. In this example they are 2, 1, and 3.

Step4: Multiply each digit by its place value.

Step5: Add these products together.

The answer in Base 10 is: 39.

Or: \( 213_{(4)} = 39_{(10)} \)
